RESULTS

Introduction

Throughout our work in the lab this summer, we were able to establish the potential viability of mutant K. phaffii strains not characterised in literature with regards to our CRISPR modifications and our growth factor constructs.

In particular, we demonstrated the potential viability of both the Hoc1 downregulation–repair modification and the PMI downregulation modification, both of which would be novel to iGEM if future testing confirms their success.

CRISPR

Show Details

We successfully transformed electrocompetent K. phaffii with sgRNA plasmids early in our experiments. Our sgRNA constructs were confirmed by gel electrophoresis, showing a single expected band around 250 bp.

Figure 1. Gel electrophoresis showing assembled sgRNA constructs (~250 bp).

We successfully transformed E. coli with sgRNA plasmids to verify sequence fidelity via sequencing. Below: PMI 2324 sgRNA transformed into BB3cH backbone and amplified using TOP10 E. coli.

Figure 2. Transformed E. coli colonies containing PMI 2324 sgRNA.

Six out of ten sgRNA plasmids sequenced returned as 100% correct clones, confirming successful assembly.

Figure 3. Test digest verification of sgRNA plasmid assemblies.

Our final transformation round yielded colonies on YNB–NRS plates — evidence of HR template uptake by K. phaffii. Though verification by colony PCR remains pending, this suggests potential success.

Figure 4. Colonies on YNB–NRS plates showing possible K. phaffii transformants.

Growth Factors

Show Details

Our growth factor experiments involved extensive troubleshooting. Early Golden Gate assemblies failed due to incorrect DNA molarity calculations, leading to underrepresentation of key fragments.

After correcting concentrations, we achieved successful assembly as verified by test digest.

Figure 5. Test digest showing successful Golden Gate assemblies.

Following transformation into K. phaffii, we obtained Zeocin-resistant colonies, though initial plates showed fungal contamination.

Figure 6. IGF–Rho transformants showing fungal contamination on YNB–Zeo plates.

Re-streaking successfully isolated uncontaminated K. phaffii colonies.

Figure 7. Re-streaked IGF–Rho transformants showing isolated K. phaffii.

Though sequence verification is pending, these strains likely express recombinant human growth factors upon methanol induction.

Bioreactor

Show Details

Due to the lack of characterised transformants, we conducted a baseline bioreactor run using K. phaffii PN-2 to collect reference data on nutrient flow and culture dynamics.

Figure 8. Bioreactor foaming observed during K. phaffii PN-2 baseline run.

The 55-hour run provided valuable process insights for future fermentation optimisation. We are continuing to analyse supernatant composition and evaluate potential protein accumulation.

BioKernel Validation Experiment

Show Details

To validate our cloning workflow, we used the EcoFlex assembly system for our enzymes of interest. Colony PCR confirmed a 65% assembly success rate (50/76 colonies positive).

Figure 9. Colony PCR of EcoFlex assembly demonstrating 50/76 successful insertions.

Though Stage 3 cloning was not completed before wiki freeze, these results validate BioKernel’s design and will guide future optimisation of the astaxanthin pathway in E. coli.
